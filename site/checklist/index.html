Trader's Process Checklist

Before every trade. One page. No shortcuts.

Use this checklist before every sim or live trade. A process that passes all five gates is a process you can trust — regardless of outcome.

Education, not financial advice. Trading involves risk of significant loss. Simulated results are not real trading. No signals, ever. — TradeGame

Before the Trade

  • SETUP What is the market condition?
    Is it trending, ranging, choppy, or low-volume? Write it down before you look at entry levels. Do not enter if you cannot name the condition.
  • RISK What is my account-risk percentage for this trade?
    Max 1-2% of simulation account on any single position. Calculate the dollar amount before you open the trade.
  • STOP Where is my stop loss, and is it placed before entry?
    The stop goes in before the order. Not after. If the stop is not set before you click buy/sell, the trade does not happen.
  • SIZE Does my position size match my risk rule?
    Position size = (account × risk %) / (entry − stop distance in dollars). Do the math. If the result feels too small, the position is the right size.
  • INTENT Why am I taking this trade?
    One sentence: "Because [specific condition] aligns with my [specific rule]." If you cannot write the sentence, do not enter.

During the Trade

  • MONITOR Is the trade still within my conditions?
    Re-check at a defined interval (e.g., every 15m, every candle close). If conditions have changed, reassess — do not hold out of hope.
  • HOLD Am I watching the P&L and feeling emotional?
    If you feel the urge to cancel your stop, move your target, or add size: stop. Step away. Let the stop do its job.
  • ADJUST Have my risk parameters been violated?
    Only move a stop to tighten (reduce risk) — never to widen. A moved stop is a new position. Re-run the Before checklist if you widen.

After the Trade

  • DEBRIEF Did I follow my rules — not did I win or lose?
    Journal the answer before you look at the P&L. Correct process × losing trade = full learning value. Lucky win × bad process = flag it.
  • JOURNAL What did the process reveal?
    Write one thing you would do the same and one thing you would change. No outcome language. "I followed my stop rule" not "I should have held longer."
  • RESET What is my state before the next trade?
    If I am chasing a loss, celebrating a win, or feeling urgent about the next setup: stop. The sim will be there tomorrow.

Bonus — Red Flag Quick-Check

  • FLAG Am I sizing up because I feel "sure" about this one?
    Confidence is not a sizing input. Your risk rule is. If you are deviating from the rule because of certainty, you are gambling.
  • FLAG Am I taking this trade because I need to recover a loss?
    Revenge trading is the fastest way to blow up a sim account. Walk away for 15 minutes. If the setup is still there when you come back, re-run the checklist from the top.
  • FLAG Am I bored and just want to do something?
    "Do nothing, journal why" earns more XP in TradeGame than an impulsive trade. Patience is a skill.
